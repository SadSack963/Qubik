# Qubik
A modern vision of the classic "Qubic" created in 1964 by Parker Brothers. 
For all you Gen Z and later, this was an actual physical 3D Tic-Tac-Toe game that you could hold in your hands!

I first played a version of this game using 4 x 4 grids hand drawn on sheets of paper! We used to make our own entertainment in those days. ;) 

I spent many happy hours playing this game, Risk and Contract Bridge with a group of friends from the Physics Department at the University of Western Australia.

Anyway, I've been thinking about this game on and off for the last 50 years. Finally at the age of 70 (!), and thanks in no small part to the AI revolution, I have achieved a working version in my favourite programming language (thanks Guido) that is quite similar to what I have been visualizing in my head all this time.

I have searched for similar implementations on the internet. There are plenty that simulate using the standard four separate grids, and there's even a physical version where you drop small blocks into a cube with clear plasic sides. But I have not seen a truly 3D version. Surely, I cannot be the first?

This version is designed to be played on a PC using keyboard and mouse (I'm old school guys). I have no doubt it could be easily converted to a Flutter or JavaScript app.

I'm sure the graphics could be vastly improved, especially using a half-decent game engine, but I wanted to get the concept out there before I kick the bucket! Maybe in the future somebody else will pick this little script up and improve on it.

The rules are simple - get four of your pieces in a line before your opponent does - like Connect Four, only in 3 dimensions.

**_Enjoy_**
